(A) Osteochondrosis of the tibial tuberosity
(B) More commonly found in adult than adolescents
(C) Fragmental on of the tibial tuberosity becomes apparent after 3 or 4 weeks after injury.
(D) Patient present with pain, so tissue swelling and tenderness in the anterior knee
??
**Ans: B**
Osgood-Schlatter disease (tibial osteochondrosis) is seen in active adolescents, especially those who jump and kick, usually affecting boys between ages 10-15 years.
