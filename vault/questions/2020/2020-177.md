#交換 #2020交換 #Unknown Which one is **wrong** about focal fat-sparing in diffuse fatty liver?
(A) Characteristic locations include: in porta hepatis, next to gallbladder bed, adjacent to falciform ligament / ligamentum venosum, and subcapsular skip areas
(B) No mass effect
(C) Contrast enhancement similar to normal liver
(D) Decrease in signal intensity on opposed-phase gradient-echo T1WI
??
**Ans: D**
Pseudolesions (focal sparing) are better seen on out-of-phase imaging (**lack of decrease** in signal intensity on opposed-phase gradient-echo T1WI), but otherwise, appear normal and similar to the rest of the liver on T2 and contrast-enhanced sequences.
Important features are:
- absence of distortion of vessels that run through the region
- absence of mass effect
