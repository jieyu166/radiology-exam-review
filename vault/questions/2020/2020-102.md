#交換 #2020交換 #MSK Regarding limb length discrepancy, which of the following is correct?
(A) Various diseases can cause limb length discrepancy, such as tibia or fibula hemimelia, Proteus syndrome, etc.
(B) Physis disruption caused by trauma or infection during adulthood is the common cause the limb length discrepancy.
(C) Limb length discrepancy of greater than 6mm is used as a standard landmark for intervention.
(D) A set of conventional scanography includes at least three radiographs, which are taken with centers at hip joint, knee joint and sacroiliac joints respectively.
??
**Ans: A**
- Limb length discrepancy, or anisomelia, may be
	- secondary to congenital disorders such as tibia or fibula hemimelia, Proteus syndrome, and idiopathic hemihypertrophy;
	- secondary to paralytic disorders such as cerebral palsy and polio;
	- secondary to physis disruption such as that caused by trauma, infection, neoplasm, or irradiation.
- B. ~~adult~~ --> childhood
- C. 通常>2cm才會處理. Usually, a limb length discrepancy of greater than 2 cm is used as a landmark for intervention.
- At conventional scanography, three radiographs are taken: one centered at the hip joint, one at the knee, and one at the ~~sacroiliac~~ --> ankle in the frontal plane. [[Orthoroentgenogram]]
