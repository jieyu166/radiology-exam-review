#交換 #2020交換 #Unknown Which one is **wrong** about ectopic pancreas?
(A) Most common heterotopia within GI system
(B) The most common location is gastric lesser curvature
(C) May show central umbilication
(D) Submucosa in one half
??
**Ans: B**
- presence of pancreatic tissue in the submucosal, muscularis or subserosal layers of the luminal GI tract outside the normal confines of the pancreas and lacking any anatomic or vascular connection with the main pancreas.
- 45% of the cases of ectopic pancreas discovered on upper gastrointestinal examination, the ectopic pancreatic tissue contains a central small collection of barium, i.e. a central niche or umbilication, indicative of the rudimentary duct’s draining orifice.On upper gastrointestinal examination, an ectopic pancreas appears as an extramucosal, smooth, broad-based lesion either along the greater curvature of the gastric antrum or in the proximal duodenum.
