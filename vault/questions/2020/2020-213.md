#交換 #2020交換 #CV About alternative access beyond the femoral arterial approach for transcatheter aortic valve replacement (TAVR), which description is **wrong**?
(A) An abnormal location of the left ventricular apex in the thorax (too medial, inferior, or posterior) is not a relative contraindication for a transapical approach.
(B) The factors that may prevent a subclavian approach include a narrowed vessel (<6 mm), severe calcification, and excessive tortuosity.
(C) In the transaortic approach, the ideal aortotomy site is located along the right lateral aspect of the aorta, along the greater curvature of the aorta.
(D) Preexisting pleural calcifications and pericardial calcifications may limit access to the left ventricular apex via thoracotomy.
??
**Ans: A**
左心室尖端異常位置（過於medial、inferior或posterior）為transapical approach之相對禁忌症，並非「非」相對禁忌症。Transapical approach為最侵襲性之替代路徑，通常保留給股動脈、鎖骨下動脈、transaortic路徑皆有禁忌時採用；限制left ventricular apex經thoracotomy路徑之因素包括：左心室尖端位置異常、既往心肌梗塞造成之心肌變薄或鈣化、肋膜/心包鈣化、左心室射血分率過低(<20%)。Subclavian approach禁忌因素：血管狹窄(<6mm)、嚴重鈣化、過度扭曲。Transaortic approach理想aortotomy位置：主動脈右側緣、沿主動脈greater curvature，距離無名動脈近端1cm，距離主動脈瓣至少5.5cm。
