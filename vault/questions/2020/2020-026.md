#交換 #2020交換 #ABD Concerning adrenal myelolipomas, which one is **true**?
(A) they are composed of fat and muscle tissue
(B) they can occasionally be functioning tumors
(C) they should be removed surgically as they are premalignant
(D) they can be distinguished readily from other adrenal masses on CT because of their fat content.
(E) They are occasionally seen in patient with tuberous sclerosis
??
**Ans: D**
Histological examination demonstrates variable amounts of:
- mature adipocytes (with distended lipid vacuoles) similar to bone marrow
- hematopoietic cells (including cells from myeloid, erythroid and megakaryocytic cell lines)
Although the tumor itself is non-functioning, but relatively high incidence (10%) of associated endocrine disorders:
- Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (21-hydroxylase deficiency)
- Conn syndrome (primary hyperaldosteronism)
- Cushing syndrome
They are benign lesions histologically and there is currently no recognized malignant potential.
In larger lesions (**>4cm**) or where hemorrhage has occurred surgical excision is curative.
If imaging features are characteristic and the lesion is small, no treatment is required.
