#交換 #2020交換 #NR Which case would less likely find lesion at bilateral globi pallidi in brain MRI?
(A) 34 years old man with methanol poisoning
(B) 26 years old man escaped from fire with carbon monoxide poisoning
(C) 22 years old woman conscious disturbance after intravenous heroin overdose
(D) 54 years old man with cirrhosis and severe impaired liver function for many years
??
**Ans: A**
methanol poisoning. Selective or predominant involvement of the putamina with relative **sparing** of the globi pallidi is suggestive of methanol poisoning
- CO poisoning: involving both globi pallidi and sparing the putamina, a classic finding of this condition.
- IV heroin overdose: most common acute complication of injected heroin is stroke, typically involving the globi pallidi(\~10% of cases), which has a similar imaging manifestation in cases of carbon monoxide poisoning.
- Chronic hepatic encephlopathy:T1 hyperintensity involving the globi pallidi and substantiae nigrae (reported in 80%–90% of patients with chronic liver failure), most likely due to manganese accumulation. High signal intensity on T1-weighted images of the pituitary gland and the hypothalamus can be depicted but is less common.
