(A) 2-5% of all primary bone tumors but cause almost 40% of clival tumor.

(C) Conventional chordoma are very hyperintense on T2WI, reflecting high fluid content.
(D) Typically arise off-midline.

??
**Ans: C**
(mildline)
Chordomas are uncommon malignant tumors of the axial skeleton that account for 1% of intracranial tumors and 4% of all primary bone tumors.
- 50% occur in sacrum
	- 40% of all sacral tumors
	- Most common in 4th or 5th sacral segment
- 35% at skull base around clivus
	- Benign tumors but may locally invade and compress
- 15% in remainder of spine
	- Cervical spine most common
Chordomas present as firm masses. Fluid and gelatinous mucoid substance (associated with recent and old hemorrhage) and necrotic areas are found within these tumors -> T2: most exhibit very high signal.
Prognosis is typically poor due to the locally aggressive nature of these tumors. Metastatic spread of chordomas is observed in 7-14% of patients and includes nodal, pulmonary, bone, cerebral or abdominal visceral involvement, predominantly from massive tumors.
