#交換 #2020交換 #ABD Concerning hepatic iron overload, which one of the following is incorrect?
(A) On unenhanced CT, normal liver attenuation ranges from 45 to 65 HU.
(B) Iron overload can result in a homogeneously hyperattenuating liver (75 to 135 HU) because of the high electron density associated with iron atoms.
(C) Iron overload causes T2 and T2* shortening of the liver, which manifests as relative hypointensity on T2W and T2*W images.
(D) Spin-echo sequences (T2 relaxation) are more sensitive to mild iron overload than Gradient-echo sequences (T2* relaxation).
??
**Ans: D**
應為Gradient-echo序列(T2*弛豫)對輕度鐵過載較spin-echo序列(T2弛豫)敏感，而非相反。肝內鐵具順磁性，使T2弛豫時間縮短，此加速弛豫程度與鐵含量成正比；需以不同echo time取得訊號才能得到可靠T2測量值，訊號強度對echo time之函數關係可製成T2 parametric map。CT雖易取得，但對hemochromatosis診斷敏感度不佳；陽性病例可見肝密度均勻性增加(75-130 HU)，使門脈與肝靜脈相對於肝實質呈低衰減；dual-energy CT可用於定量鐵沉積。
