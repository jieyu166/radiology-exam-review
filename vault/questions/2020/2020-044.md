#交換 #2020交換 #ABD Which of the following descriptions about paraduodenal pancreatitis (groove pancreatitis) is **wrong**?
(A) Patients with groove pancreatitis are inevitably middle-aged men with a history of significant alcohol abuse.
(B) Biochemical markers are great help: Pancreatic enzymes are often highly elevated, and tumor markers (e.g., CEA and CA199) are usually positive.
(C) The treatment is usually supportive care, and some patients may require a classic or pylorus-sparing pancreaticoduodenectomy (Whipple procedure) as a result of severe pancreatic insufficiency, weight loss, or intractable pain symptoms.
(D) The image findings includes: a “sheetlike” curvilinear crescentic shape infiltrations or frank soft tissue in the groove between the pancreatic head and duodenum, with increasing delayed enhancement; thickening of perifocal duodenal wall; cysts in both the duodenal wall and pancreaticoduodenal groove.
??
**Ans: B**
- Slight elevation of serum markers: pancreatic enzymes (amylase and lipase), cholestatic liver enzymes gamma-glutamyltransferase (GGT) and alkaline phosphatase (ALP)
- Usually unremarkable level of tumor markers: carcinoembryonic antigen (CEA), CA 19-9
