#交換 #2020交換 #MSK About ischiofemoral impingement, which one is **wrong**?
(A) Painful compression of quadratus femoris muscle between ischial tuberosity and lesser trochanter
(B) Deep gluteal pain major complaint with specific clinical tests
(C) Normal ischiofemoral space is 23 ± 8 mm
(D) Normal quadratus femoris space is 12 ± 4 mm
??
**Ans: B**
- ischiofemoral space: cut-off ≤15 mm
- quadratus femoris space: cut-off ≤10 mm
![ADD IFI](data/images/obsidian/add-ifi-c5ab12ba.png)
- Most patients present with hip and/or groin pain. Distal radiation of pain to the posterior thigh and knee is not uncommon and is likely due to irritation of the adjacent sciatic nerve.
- Chronic low back pain is common.
- Patients may report a snapping sensation, locking of the joint and crepitation.
- On physical examination, most patients have pain during hip range of motion. Symptoms may be reproduced by a combination of hip extension, adduction and external rotation or with flexion and internal rotation. This pain is usually localized to the posterior hip.
