#交換 #2020交換 #CH About wall thickening of a cystic airspace, which description is not correct?
(A) Progressive wall thickening should be regard as a sign that the focus need further surveillance.
(B) Nodularity at wall of a cystic airspace at the interface of the normal and the fibrotic lung raises suspicious for malignancy.
(C) Lack of an uptake of fluorine 18 fluorodeoxyglucose could not exclude malignancy.
(D) It is pathognomonic of lung cancer
??
**Ans: D**
![ADD lung cavity types](data/images/obsidian/add-lung-cavity-types-e6bc2597.png) [[2018 RG Lung Cancers Associated with Cystic Airspaces - Underrecognized Features of Early Disease.pdf]]
- Type I and type IV cystic airspace-related cancers are more likely to be miscategorized as benign
lesions,
- Types II and III have a broader range of differential diagnoses, including infection and inflammation.
- For findings potentially related to infection, follow-up imaging is usually sufficient.
- For more suspicious or persistent lesions, a negative FDG PET result is not necessarily reassuring unless there is a **sizeable (>10 mm) solid component**
