About Lemierre's syndrome, which of the following statement is **wrong**?
(A) Also known as postanginal sepsis and necrobacillosis, is the suppurative thrombophlebitis of jugular vein.
(B) The condition is frequently preceded by pharyngitis, usually in association with tonsil or peritonsillar involvement.
(C) The causative organisms are usually members of the normal oropharyngeal flora; the most common is Fusobacterium necrophorum
(D) Imaging hallmark include pharyngitis, infectious lymphadenitis, and thrombosis of jugular vein. Septic emboli to lungs are rare.
??
**Ans: D**
> [!todo] 待補詳解
> Bacteremia and distal infective thromboembolism is common (lungs most commonly affected). A significant proportion of patients (13% to 27%) will require diagnostic arthrocentesis due to symptoms of septic arthritis. Meningitis has also been shown to complicate up to 3% of cases.
