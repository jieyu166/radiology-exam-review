#交換 #2016交換 #NR 關於 MRI，下列何項敘述為非？ Chemical shift is due to the differences between resonance frequencies of fat and water. It occurs in the frequency-encode direction Type 1, or chemical shift misregistration artifact, is present on all sequences to some extent, although it is often imperceptible. Type 2, or chemical shift cancellation artifact, is only present on out of phase sequences
(A) Type 2 chemical shift artifact, or chemical shift cancellation artifact, is present only on out-of-phase images when there is a proportion of both fat and water signal in a pixel.
(B) Areas that are composed solely of fat completely lose intensity on out-of-phase images.
(C) The India ink artifact is the thin black line that completely lines the abdominal organ on all sides, differentiating it from type 1 chemical shift artifact, which only occurs along the frequency encoding axis.
(D) The liver and spleen can appear brighter on out-of-phase images in the setting of hemosiderosis as a result of increased T2* effects on the in-phase.
??
**Ans: B**
反相位訊號抵消需『同一像素內脂＋水並存』,純脂肪不會完全失訊號,故 (B) 為非。
(A) Type2 只見於反相位(脂水並存):正確。
(B) **純脂肪在 out-of-phase 完全失訊號(正解＝為非)**:抵消需脂+水並存。
(C) India ink 環繞器官、與 type1(僅頻率編碼軸)區分:正確。
(D) Hemosiderosis 時肝脾 out-of-phase 較亮(in-phase T2*):正確。
