#交換 #2016交換 #MSK A 25-year-old male with a 5-month history of increasing low back pain. No other neurological symptoms or signs. An x-ray of lumbar spine demonstrates an enlarged sclerotic left pedicle of L3. A subsequent CT scan shows a 3 cm lucent focus within the left pedicle of L3, which has expanded the bone with surrounding sclerosis. What is the most likely diagnosis?
(A) Osteoid osteoma
(B) Osteoblastoma
(C) Intracortical hemangioma
(D) Enostosis
??
**Ans: B**
**病灶3cm已超過osteoid osteoma典型上限（<1.5-2cm），且為膨脹性病灶伴周圍硬化**，故最可能為osteoblastoma。
(A) 錯：osteoid osteoma典型<1.5-2cm，本病灶3cm過大。
(B) **對（正解）**：osteoblastoma好發脊椎後柱結構（含椎弓根），體積較osteoid osteoma大（通常>1.5-2cm），可持續增大並使骨膨脹伴周圍硬化。
(C) 錯：intracortical hemangioma罕見於此表現。
(D) 錯：enostosis（骨島）為緻密骨島，非膨脹性溶骨病灶。
