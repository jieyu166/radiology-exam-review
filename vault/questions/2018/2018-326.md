#交換 #2018交換 #IR Which of the following description about cryoablation is NOT **true**?
(A) The mechanism of cell death with freezing is associated with cell membrane disruption and an associated release of intracellular contents.
(B) The temperature necessary to cause reliable cellular necrosis depends on the cell type and thermal history of the tissue, with estimates of -35°C to -20°C
(C) The bleeding risk of cryoablation for patients with liver cirrhosis and hepatocellular carcinoma obviously equals to that of heat ablation.
(D) The precision that is possible with cryoablation can be important for treatment of tumors adjacent to vulnerable structures
??
**Ans: C**
Heat is the preferred method for treating HCC in patients with cirrhosis because of the increased bleeding risk and disseminated intravascular coagulation-like reaction, called cryoshock, associated with cryoablation. Heat-based ablation provides improved hemostasis compared with cryoablation and essentially no risk for cryoshock; 實際上cryoablation出血風險高於heat ablation，非相等。
