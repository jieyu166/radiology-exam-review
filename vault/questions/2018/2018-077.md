#交換 #2018交換 #NR A 12 year-old boy presents with a painless swelling mass in his right neck, at the angle of mandible. Ultrasound shows a cystic lesion with internal debris. CT confirms a cystic lesion with a beak of tissue pointing between internal and external carotid arteries. What’s the most likely diagnosis?
(A) cystic hygroma
(B) 2nd branchial cleft cyst
(C) 3rd branchial cleft cyst
(D) carotid body tumor
(E) lymphangioma
??
**Ans: B**
- Most third branchial cleft cysts tend to lie in the posterior cervical space, posterior to the sternocleidomastoid muscle
- Cystic hygroma (cystic/nuchal lymphangioma) most commonly occur in the cervicofacial regions (posterior cervical triangle)
