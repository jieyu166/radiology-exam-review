#交換 #2018交換 #MSK Which is **FALSE** about bisphosphonate-related atypical femur fractures (AFF)?
(A) Non-comminuted or minimally comminuted
(B) Localized periosteal or endosteal thickening of the lateral cortex at the fracture site
(C) Associated with minimal or no trauma
(D) Fracture line is substantially transverse which may become oblique
(E) Mostly occurs at femoral neck
??
**Ans: E**
The atypical fracture pattern occurs in the **proximal third** of the femur, typically **subtrochanteric**, and may be unilateral or bilateral. Atypical femoral fractures can also occur more inferiorly to the level of the supracondylar region.
Atypical Subtrochanteric and Diaphyseal Femoral Fractures: Second Report of a Task Force of the American Society for Bone and Mineral Research Shane E, JBMR 2014, 29;1-23
