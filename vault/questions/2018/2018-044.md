#交換 #2018交換 #CH What pulmonary feature is most suggestive of pulmonary infarction in the context of acute [[Pulmonary embolism]]?
(A) Reverse halo sign
(B) Focal airspace opacity with air-bronchogram
(C) Mosaic lung attenuation
(D) Segmental atelectasis
??
**Ans: A**



## Reference
[^1]: wedge-shaped (less often rounded) juxtapleural opacification (Hampton hump) without air bronchograms
[^2]: consolidation with internal air lucencies, "bubbly consolidation"
[^3]: halo sign: secondary to adjacent hemorrhage
[^4]: reverse halo sign: low attenuation within the lesion (necrosis)
[^5]: cavitation: may be seen in septic embolism and infection of a bland infarct
