(A) The first and easiest view to obtain is of the right-upper quadrant hepatorenal space, also known as Morison's pouch.
(B) The goal of the FAST examination is to visualize intraperitoneal fluid in dependent areas of the abdominal cavity.
(C) Intraperitoneal blood is anechoic (black) on ultrasonography.
(D) Ultrasonography has a high sensitivity for detecting parenchymal injury of liver or kidney.
??
