#交換 #2018交換 #NR A 55 years old man presents with morning headaches and bitemporal hemianopsia. The head MRI shows a supra-sellar solid mass with small cyst component, and vivid enhancement of solid component after contrast administration. Which one of the following is the most likely diagnosis?
(A) Craniopharyngioma
(B) Rathke cleft cyst
(C) Pituitary macroadenoma
(D) Intracranial teratoma
??
**Ans: A**
雖然沒提到鈣化。但年紀大papillary較多，罕見鈣化。 [[ADD DDx hypothalamic lesions.png]]
- B. Rathke cleft cyst: cyst T2亮 T1看成分，不會顯影
- C. Pituitary macroadenoma 可能有solid, cystic part, necrosis, hemorrhage可能不容易區分。Fluid-fluid level比較常見在adenomas，而Craniopharyngioma, Rathke cleft cyst 較少這樣
- D. Teratoma還要看到fat, 鈣化。
