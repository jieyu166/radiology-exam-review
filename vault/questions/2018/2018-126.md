#交換 #2018交換 #2019交換 #Unknown Pelvic congestion syndrome (PCS) is a cause of chronic pelvic pain, its typical image finding includes all of the following except
(A) dilated, torturous, enhancing tubular structures near the uterus and ovary.
(B) on T1-weighted images, varices may appear as flow voids.
(C) Ultrasound shows slow (less than 3cm/s) venous flow in cranial direction in left ovarian vein.
(D) more than 50% of women with PCS have associated cystic ovaries.
??
**Ans: C**
Criteria for the sonographic diagnosis of varices includes:
- (1) the visualization of dilated ovarian veins **>4 mm** in diameter,
- (2) dilated tortuous arcuate veins in the myometrium that communicate with bilateral pelvic varicose veins,
- (3) **slow** blood flow (**< 3 cm/s**), and reversed caudal or **retrograde** venous blood flow particularly in the left ovarian vein
- CT: parauterine vein > 4mm, gonadal vein > 8mm
US or Venogram: gonadal vein > 5mm
