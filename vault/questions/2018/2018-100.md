#交換 #2018交換 #PED Which of the following about congenial ureteral anomalies is correct?
(A) In most case of VUR children, a long distal ureteric submucosal tunnel in the bladder can be found.
(B) Primary megaureter can be caused by VUR.
(C) Ectopic ureter often insertion to more cephalic place of bladder, for example, bladder dome.
(D) In patient with duplex collecting system, a ureterocele often obstructed the lower pole moiety.
??
**Ans: B**
- A: short distal ureteric submucosal tunnel in the bladder
- C: Consequently, the opening of the ureter becomes caudally ectopic and, in the female, inserts into the lower bladder, urethra, vestibule, or vagina.
- D: obstruction of the upper pole moiety down to the bladder, often with a ureterocoele
