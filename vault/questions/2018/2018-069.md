#交換 #2018交換 #2019交換 #2021交換 #2022交換 #2023交換 #MSK An MRI appearance of homogeneous low T1-and T2-weighted signal with enhancement is **MOST** typical of which foot and ankle mass?
(A) Lipoma.
(B) Ganglion cyst.
(C) Plantar fibromatosis.
(D) Hemangioma.
(E) Morton's neuromas.
??
**Ans: C**
- T1 跟T2都 homogeneously低訊號，想到fibrous tissue。most commonly seen in fibrous masses containing **mature** collagen.
	 - Fibrosing masses containing immature fibrous tissue or fibroblasts can have intermediate signal intensity.
	 - Fibrosing masses include plantar fibromatosis and fibroma of tendon sheath. **Option C is the best response.**
 - Morton's neuromas typically have intermediate signal that is **isointense to muscle on T1**-weighted and **low signal on T2**-weighted sequences with variable enhancement (RadioGraphics 1998; 18:1481 –1498)
 - Lipomas: fat signal intensity. They typically have **high T1-and T2**-weighted signal and low signal on **fat-suppressed** sequences.
 - Ganglion cyst: The typical appearance of a ganglion cyst on MRI is a well-defined mass with **low T1-and high T2**-weighted signal.
	 - Uncomplicated ganglion cysts do not have central enhancement, although a thin rim of enhancement may surround the ganglion.
	 - If a suspected ganglion cyst has **central enhancement, then malignancy must be excluded**.
 - Hemangiomas have **mixed** signal on T1-and T2-weighted sequences because of the presence of vessels, fat, and fibrous tissue (Semin Musculoskelet Radiol 2005; 9:227 –242).
	 - The vascular portions of hemangiomas homogeneously enhance.
	 - Densely calcified masses can have low T1-and T2-weighted signal, but would not be expected to enhance.
