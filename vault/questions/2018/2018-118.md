#交換 #2018交換 #Unknown Which of the following is **not** a common location of osteochondritis dissecans?
(A) Femoral condyles
(B) Trochlear of the humerus
(C) Talus
(D) Patella
??
**Ans: B**
1. knee(75%): femoral condyles are most common site
   - medial condyle:  "classic" lateral surface of the medial condyle: ~70%
   - lateral condyle: 15%; inferocentral portion: 13%; anterior surface: 2%
   - patella: ~7.5% (range 5-10%) typically inferomedial
   - patellofemoral groove (femoral trochlea): <1%
   - weight-bearing surface of the tibia: uncommon
