#交換 #2019交換 #CV About coronary artery anomalies, which of the following descriptions is **wrong**?
(A) In multiple ostia, typically either the RCA and the conus branch arise separately or the LAD and LCx arteries arise separately with no LCA.
(B) "High takeoff" refers to the origin of coronary artery at a point above the junctional zone between its sinus and the tubular part of the ascending aorta.
(C) The most common course of an anomalous RCA arising from the left sinus of Valsalva is prepulmonic.
(D) An interarterial course carries a high risk for sudden cardiac death.
??
**Ans: **
異常 RCA 起源於 left sinus of Valsalva 時，最常見路徑為 interarterial（介於主動脈與肺動脈之間），而非 prepulmonic。
