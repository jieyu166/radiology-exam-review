#交換 #2019交換 #2022交換 #CH Which of the following is NOT characterized by multiple small 1- to 5-mm pulmonary nodules?
(A) Metastases
(B) Asbestosis
(C) Tuberculosis
(D) Silicosis
(E) Langerhans cell histiocytosis
??
**Ans: B**
Ref: Chest Radiology: The Essentials 3rd edition, Chapter 3. page 396; Radiopaedia.
- Pleural effusions and pleural plaques are common manifestations of asbestos-related disease,
- Hilar and mediastinal LAP is usually absent. (Radiopaedia: can sometimes present) DDx: mesothelioma
- Appearances of asbestosis vary with the duration and severity of the condition.
- Early manifestations are largely confined to the peripheral region of the lower zones and are subtle. They include:
	- centrilobular dot-like opacities: peribronchial fibrosis
	- intralobular linear opacities: reticulation
	- subpleural lines (often curvilinear)
- As the fibrosis progresses, a number of more definite findings are seen, which continue to be particularly subpleural and lower lung zone in distribution. They include:
	- parenchymal bands
	- traction bronchiectasis
	- honeycomb fibrosis
