#交換 #2019交換 #Unknown Which one is **WRONG** about vein of Galen aneurysmal malformation?
(A) In neonate, it could cause high-output congestive heart failure.
(B) Large midline varix in neonate/infant.
(C) Embryonic falcine sinus drains the midline varix in 50%.
(D) Common arterial feeder is anterior choroidal artery.
??
**Ans: D**
Feeders: posterior choroidal arteries, anterior cerebral arteries, thalamic perforating arteries, and superior cerebellar arteries.
present dramatically during early childhood with features of a left-to-right shunt and high-output cardiac failure.
 Venous drainage is via the median prosencephalic vein (MPV), the straight sinus (if present) and then out via the transverse/sigmoid sinuses.
The anomaly is actually due to a cerebral arteriovenous fistula of the median prosencephalic vein (MPV) (a precursor of the vein of Galen) occurring at 6-11 weeks gestation and not a malformation (no nidus present). The MPV fails to regress and becomes aneurysmal. It drains via the straight sinus (present only in 50%) or a persistent falcine sinus, and the vein of Galen does not form.
Lasjaunias classification: choroidal and mural types
- choroidal type:present earlier (neonate) with more severe shunts
- mural type: present later (infant) and typically with hydrocephalus
