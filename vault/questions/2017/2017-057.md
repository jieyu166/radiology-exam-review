#交換 #2017交換 #NR A 12 year-old boy presents with a painless swelling mass in his right neck, at the angle of mandible. Ultrasound shows a cystic lesion with internal debris. CT confirms a cystic lesion with a beak of tissue **pointing between internal and external carotid arteries**. What’s the most likely diagnosis?
(A) cystic hygroma
(B) abscess
(C) branchial cleft cyst
(D) carotid body tumor
(E) lymphadenopathy
??
**Ans: C**
**C（branchial cleft cyst）**——beak sign（組織尖端指向內外頸動脈分岔之間）為第二鰓裂囊腫的高度特異定位徵象，好發下頷角無痛性囊性腫塊。
(A) Cystic hygroma:多房性,常跨頸部多區域。
(B) Abscess:臨床常伴疼痛發炎徵象。
(C) **正解**:beak sign指向頸動脈分岔間。
(D) Carotid body tumor:富血管實質腫塊,使頸動脈分岔外擴。
(E) Lymphadenopathy:非典型囊性伴beak sign表現。
