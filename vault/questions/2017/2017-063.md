#交換 #2017交換 #NR Regarding to Ludwig’s angina, which of the following description is **wrong**?
(A) It is a life-threatening cellulitis of mouth floor.
(B) Transpatial extension to submandibular, sublingual, and submental space is common.
(C) MRI is the modality of choice.
(D) Most cases originate from odontogenic infection.
??
**Ans: C**
CT.
- Preceding dental infection.
- mainly Streptococcus spp., Staphylococcus spp., and Bacteroides spp.
- spread of infection can occur between these spaces: submandibular, sublingual, and submental spaces and the mylohyoid muscle.
- Subsequent swelling can displace the tongue superiorly and posteriorly leading to potential airway obstruction and aspyhyxiation.
