#交換 #2017交換 #CV Which statement is **false** regarding coronary artery fistulas (CAFs)?
(A) The clinical presentation is mainly dependent on the severity of the left-to-right shunt. The majority of adult patients are usually asymptomatic.
(B) The majority of symptomatic CAFs originate from the RCA, but asymptomatic CAFs demonstrate a greater prevalence of origin from the LCA.
(C) Low-pressure structures are the most common sites of drainage of the CAF.
(D) The most common drainage site is pulmonary artery
??
**Ans: D**
- The most common drainage sites in order of decreasing frequency are the right ventricle (41%), right atrium (26%), pulmonary artery (17%) , coronary sinus (7%), left atrium (5%), left ventricle (3%), and superior vena cava (1%)
- The most common origins for the CAVF are the RCA (50%) and LAD (42%), and both the RCA and LCA in approximately 5% of patients
- A smaller percentage of pediatric patients tend to be asymptomatic, unlike adults
- These drainage sites may not be well-visualized at conventional angiography because of significant dilution of the contrast medium. Magnetic resonance (MR) imaging and CT are useful, noninvasive, and accurate imaging techniques
