#交換 #2017交換 #CV Which of the following statements is **TRUE** concerning MRI of cardiomyopathy?
(A) In nonischemic dilated cardiomyopathy, there is regional ventricular wall thinning.
(B) In hypertrophic cardiomyopathy, the left ventricle is grossly dilated.
(C) Restrictive cardiomyopathy can be distinguished from constrictive pericarditis by MRI.
(D) Blooming decreased myocardial signal intensity on gradient recalled echo (GRE) sequences suggests amyloidosis.
??
**Ans: C**
- A. The left ventricular wall is rather thin. However, as the left ventricle is enlarged, the total left ventricular mass may be increased.
	- In ischemic dilated cardiomyopathy, regional ventricular wall thinning occurs in one or more locations;
	- In nonischemic dilated cardiomyopathy, the ventricular wall thickness is uniform
- B. Wall thickening
- C.
	- Restrictive cardiomyopathy : Biatrial enlargement with minimal or no ventricular enlargement may be present. Cine MRI will show **altered diastolic filling**.
	- Constrictive pericarditis: there can be **septal flattening** on cine MRI and the **pericardium** can be thicker than 4 mm
	- 臨床上不容易區分
- D. Amyloidosis 沒提到會
- Late enhancement MR images may demonstrate areas of fibrosis within the myocardium, characteristically in the mid- or subepicardial myocardium, allowing differentiation from ischemic cardiomyopathy.
