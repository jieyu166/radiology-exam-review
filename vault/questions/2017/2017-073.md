#交換 #2017交換 #PED About the emergent pediatric US findings listed below, which one is **false**?
(A) The most accurate US finding for acute appendicitis is an outer wall diameter greater than 6 mm under compression.
(B) Less sensitive and specific US findings for appendicitis include hyperemia within the appendiceal wall on color Doppler images, echogenic inflamed periappendiceal fat, and the presence of an appendicolith.
(C) Longitudinal images of intussusceptions can be obtained to confirm a bowel-within-bowel appearance. If it is imaged obliquely, the ”pseudokidney” sign may result.
(D) When measuring hypertrophic pyloric stenosis, calipers are placed at the superficial and deep borders of a single (anterior) layer of the hypoechoic pyloric muscle. No exact consensus on lower-limit muscle thickness has been specified in the literature, but the threshold value for a diagnosis of HPS is generally greater than 2 mm
??
**Ans: D**
- Pyloric muscle thickness, i.e. diameter of a single muscular wall on a transverse image **>3 mm (most accurate)**
- pyloric transverse diameter ≥14 mm
- length, i.e. longitudinal measurement >15 mm
Emergent Pediatric US: What Every Radiologist Should Know. RadioGraphics 2012; 32:651–665
