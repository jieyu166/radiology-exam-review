#交換 #2017交換 #Unknown Which is **wrong** about multiple bile duct harmatomas (so called von Meyenburg complex)?
(A) Both intra- and extra-hepatic bile ducts were normal in this disease.
(B) It should be differentiated from metastatic liver disease and simple hepatic cysts.
(C) Liver ultrasonogrpahy shows uniformly hypoechoic lesions without cometail artifact.
(D) Liver MRI shows hypointense on T1WI, hyperintense on T2WI, and variable enhancement pattern.
??
**Ans: C**
Tiny individual hamartomas cannot be resolved ==>interpreted as diffuse heterogenous liver echotexture.
Larger hamartomas (>10 mm) may appear hypoechoic or anechoic and comet-tail artifact may be seen.
MRI usually no enhancement
 - thin peripheral rim enhancement has been described (see below), this may represent compressed normal liver parenchyma
 - ~90% of cases an occasional hamartoma can be found containing a mural nodule (fibrocollagenous stroma, see pathology) or rim that is T1 isointense and T2 intermediate, of which ~90% will enhance.
