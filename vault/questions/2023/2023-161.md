#交換 #2023交換 #Unknown Left main bronchus (LMB) narrowing occurs more frequently in patients of coarctation of the aorta (CoA) before or after arch surgery, which factors on peri-operative CT can most predict LMB compression?
(A) Increased retrosternal distance of the ascending aorta at the carina level indicates increased risk of LMB stenosis.
(B) Increased the interaortic distance index (distance between ascending aorta to descending aorta divided by the distance between sernum to vertebral body at the carina level) indicates increased risk of LMB stenosis.
(C) Increased interaortic distance between ascending aorta to descending aorta at the carina level indicates increased risk of LMB stenosis.
(D) Decreased clockwise-angle of the descending aorta related to the vertebral body at the carina level indicates increased risk of LMB stenosis.
??
**Ans: D**
Decreased clockwise-angle of the descending aorta related to the vertebral body at the carina level indicates increased risk of LMB stenosis.
