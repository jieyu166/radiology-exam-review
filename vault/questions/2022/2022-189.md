(A) Nodules that are definitively less reflective than the anterior neck muscles, which should be visible on every image, are classified as markedly hypoechoic
(B) “Tallness” refers to a nodule’s anteroposterior dimension and “width” to its transverse dimension on an axial image.
(C) A nodule’s margin, defined as the character of its interface with adjacent intra or extrathyroidal tissue, is best appreciated along its lateral border, which is orthogonal to the ultrasound beam.
(D) Macrocalcifications and peripheral calcifications rarely present diagnostic problems, as they are usually associated with at least some degree of acoustic shadowing.
