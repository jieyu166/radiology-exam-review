(A) Fractures are the first most common and skin injury/bruising are the second most injuries in child abuse.
(B) A follow-up skeletal survey performed 2 weeks after the initial skeletal survey adds information and helps in diagnosis.
(C) In infants and toddlers, physical abuse is the cause of 12-20% of fractures.
(D) Classic metaphyseal lesions are highly specific for child abuse. It is resulted from stress at the subperiosteal bone collar, which causes a planar fracture through the primary spongiosa, separating it from the metaphysis.

**Ans: A**
Fractures are the second most common injury following skin injury and bruising in victims of child abuse.
