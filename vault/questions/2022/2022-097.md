#交換 #2022交換 #CV Which of the following is **true** about noninvasive imaging for evaluating ischemic heart disease? A moderate stenosis of coronary artery (50-69% stenosis) are usually flow limiting.
(B) The best noninvasive study to function all assess coronary stenosis is coronary CT angiography followed by PET myocardial perfusion imaging.
(C) T1 mapping of cardiovascular MR is the best technique to detect left ventricular thrombus.
(D) A left ventricular pseudoaneurysm differ from left ventricular aneurysm by having narrow neck in CT and MR images.
??
**Ans: D**
正確。
A. **錯誤**。≥ 70% stenosis
B. **錯誤**。~~coronary CT angiography~~ MR stress perfusion imaging
C. **錯誤**。~~T1 mapping~~ LGE imaging
RadioGraphics 2021; 41:990–1021
- **LGE** imaging is the best technique to help detect LV thrombus (sensitivity, 88%; specificity, 99%) and can help detect **small thrombi** missed at cine MRI and echocardiography.
- In comparison with a true aneurysm, a pseudoaneurysm is much more ominous and requires surgical repair. A pseudoaneurysm is an LV rupture that is contained by scar tissue or pericardium. At imaging, it appears as an outpouching arising from the LV that typically has a narrow neck.
