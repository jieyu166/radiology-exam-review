#交換 #2022交換 #Unknown Which of the following chest radiographic findings of viral lung infection in children is **incorrect**?
(A) Dirty central peribronchial markings
(B) Depression of the diaphragm to more than ten posterior ribs
(C) Subsegmental atelectasis
(D) Predominate reticular changes in basal lungs
??
**Ans: D**
Viral pneumonia 主要表現：Centrilobular or tree-in-bud nodule, patchy GGO, bronchopneumonia (peribronchial distribution).  from [[2018 RG Radiographic and CT Features of Viral Pneumonia.pdf]]  (a) VZV pneumonia: multifocal 1–10-mm well-defined or ill-defined nodular opacity (arrows) with a surrounding halo or patchy GGO (arrowheads) in both lungs. (b) CMV Pneumonia: diffuse ill-defined patchy GGO with interlobular septal thickening (arrowheads) in both lungs. (c) Pneumonia due to HMPV shows multiple ill-defined nodules (arrows) or GGO (arrowhead) along the bronchovascular bundles in both lungs. Similar to those of HPIV pneumonia, which belongs to the same viridae. (d) Influenza A Pneumonia: shows multiple irregular areas of consolidation (arrows) along the bronchovascular bundles and diffuse GGO (arrowheads) with interlobular septal thickening in both lungs. (e) Pneumonia due to rhinovirus shows multiple ill-defined patchy areas of GGO (arrows) with interlobular septal thickening (arrowheads) in both lungs.
