#交換 #2022交換 #MSK The following descriptions about fingers and thumb, which one is **NOT** correct?
(A) Injuries to the terminal extensor tendon are termed “mallet finger”.
(B) PIP volar plate injury often occurs through hyperextension and axial compression, so-called “Jersey finger”.
(C) The tear of ulnar collateral ligament of first metacarpophalangeal joint is called “gamekeeper’s thumb”, which may result from chronic valgus stress.
(D) The adductor pollicis aponeurosis can be interposed between the torn UCL and the MCP joint of thumb, which is called “Stener lesion”, can impede ligament healing.
??
**Ans: B**
- Jammed finger: PIP volar plate injury often occurs through hyperextension and axial compression.
- Jersey finger: FDP avulsion occurs with forced extension during active DIP flexion. Most often affects the ring finger.
