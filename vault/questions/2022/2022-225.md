#交換 #2022交換 #Unknown Features of primary sclerosing cholangitis (PSC), which one is **wrong**?
(A) F>M
(B) 75% of patients have inflammatory bowel disease
(C) 2-10% of patients with ulcerative colitis have PSC
(D) The pathology showed chronic and progressive disease characterized by inflammation, destruction, and fibrosis of the intra- and extrahepatic ducts.
??
**Ans: A**
- PSC is strongly associated with **IBD**, especially UC. Thus, shares similar demographics: Young to middle-aged **males** are most frequently affected. PSC: both extra and intrahepatic bile ducts.
- PBC, primary biliary cholangitis: young **female** more frequently affected. Characteristic destruction of small **intrahepatic** bile ducts.
